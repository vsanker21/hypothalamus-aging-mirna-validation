GenAge / Human Ageing Genomic Resources (HAGR) — data use

Source: Genomics of Ageing and Rejuvenation Lab, models gene set distributed as
https://genomics.senescence.info/genes/models_genes.zip (file genage_models.csv inside the archive).

This pipeline downloads that archive into feasibility_study/data/cache/ for reproducibility.
The upstream site states that data are provided for research purposes; cite the GenAge database
and the original publications referenced therein. Do not redistribute the raw archive as your
own product; link to HAGR/GenAge instead.

Recommended citations (verify current wording on https://genomics.senescence.info/ before publication):
- Tacutu R, et al. Human Ageing Genomic Resources: new and updated databases. Nucleic Acids Res. 2018.
- de Magalhaes JP, et al. The Human Ageing Genomic Resources: online databases and tools for
  comparative studies in longevity and ageing. Nucleic Acids Res. 2009.

Interpretation: GenAge “model organism” entries are literature-curated longevity-associated genes,
not a tissue-specific hypothalamus signature. Overlap with miRNA targets is exploratory context only.
